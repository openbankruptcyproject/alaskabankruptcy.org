Bankruptcy in Anchorage, Alaska

Anchorage is Alaska's largest city and economic center, home to nearly 290,000 residents. The District of Alaska bankruptcy courthouse is located here at 605 W 4th Ave. This guide covers everything Anchorage residents need to know about filing bankruptcy.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Alaska Exemptions for Anchorage Filers

Alaska is one of the states that allows you to choose between state exemptions and federal bankruptcy exemptions. You must use one complete set -- you cannot mix and match.

Filing Bankruptcy in Anchorage

The Process

  1. Credit counseling -- Complete an approved course within 180 days before filing ($15-25).
  2. File your petition -- Filed with the District of Alaska at 605 W 4th Ave. The automatic stay stops collections, garnishments, and foreclosures immediately.
  3. 341 meeting -- About 30-40 days after filing. In Anchorage, meetings are typically held in person at the courthouse, though telephonic appearances may be available.
  4. Debtor education -- Complete a second financial course before discharge ($10-25).
  5. Discharge -- Chapter 7: ~60 days after the 341 meeting. Chapter 13: after completing your 3-5 year plan.

Cost Estimates for Anchorage

  • Chapter 7 filing fee: $338 (installment payments available)
  • Chapter 13 filing fee: $313 (can be paid through your plan)
  • Attorney fees: Typically $1,500-$3,000 for Chapter 7; $3,500-$5,500 for Chapter 13
  • Credit counseling: $15-25 per course (two required)

Alaska's higher cost of living affects attorney rates. Fee waivers are available for filers with income below 150% of the poverty line.

Alaska's Unique Geography and Bankruptcy

Alaska's vast distances present unique challenges for bankruptcy filers. The state covers 663,000 square miles, and many residents live hours or even days of travel from the Anchorage courthouse.

  • Telephonic 341 meetings: The District of Alaska regularly allows filers outside Anchorage to attend their 341 meeting of creditors by telephone or video conference. This is especially common for residents of Fairbanks, Juneau, and rural communities.
  • Virtual hearings: Court hearings may also be conducted by telephone or video, reducing the need to travel to Anchorage.
  • Single district: Unlike larger states with multiple divisions, Alaska has one bankruptcy district. All cases are administered from the Anchorage courthouse regardless of where you live.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which bankruptcy court handles Anchorage cases?

Anchorage is in the District of Alaska, the state's only federal bankruptcy district. The courthouse is at 605 W 4th Ave, Anchorage, AK 99501. All Alaska bankruptcy cases are administered from this courthouse.

Can I choose federal or state exemptions in Alaska?

Yes. Alaska allows filers to choose between state and federal bankruptcy exemptions. You must use one complete set -- you cannot mix items from both. If you have significant home equity, the state homestead exemption ($72,900) is usually better. If you rent, the federal wildcard may protect more of your personal property. Full exemptions guide.

Can I attend my 341 meeting by phone?

Yes. The District of Alaska regularly accommodates telephonic and video appearances for 341 meetings of creditors, especially for filers outside Anchorage. Contact the trustee assigned to your case to arrange a telephonic appearance.

How much does bankruptcy cost in Anchorage?

Chapter 7 filing fee is $338; Chapter 13 is $313. Attorney fees typically run $1,500-$3,000 for Chapter 7 and $3,500-$5,500 for Chapter 13 in Anchorage. Alaska's cost of living makes attorney fees somewhat higher than the national average. Full cost guide.

What is the means test threshold for Alaska?

Alaska's median income for the means test is higher than most states due to the cost of living. If your household income is below the Alaska median for your household size, you automatically qualify for Chapter 7. If above, a detailed expense calculation determines eligibility.
