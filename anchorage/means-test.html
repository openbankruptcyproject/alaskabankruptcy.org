Anchorage Means Test: Do You Qualify for Chapter 7?

The means test determines whether you can file Chapter 7 bankruptcy. Alaska's median income thresholds are among the highest in the nation, meaning more Alaskans qualify than residents of most states.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

If your annualized household income is at or below these figures, you automatically qualify for Chapter 7.

Step-by-Step Means Test

1Calculate your current monthly income (CMI)

Add all income from all sources for the six full calendar months before filing. Divide by six. Include wages, self-employment, rental income, pension. The PFD may be included - consult your attorney.

2Annualize and compare

Multiply by 12 and compare to the Alaska median for your household size. If at or below, you pass automatically.

3If above median: deduction calculation

Subtract IRS-approved expenses, secured debt payments, and priority debts. Alaska's higher IRS Local Standards reflect the cost of living.

4Disposable income test

Multiply remaining monthly disposable income by 60. If less than $9,075, you pass. If more than $15,150, you fail.

Alaska-Specific Considerations

  • Seasonal income: Many Alaskans have seasonal employment. Filing during a low-income period may help pass the means test.
  • PFD income: Courts have taken different positions on whether the PFD counts. Discuss with your attorney.
  • High cost of living: Alaska's IRS Local Standards allow higher expense deductions than most states.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the median income for Alaska?

Approximately $73,780 (1-person), $92,320 (2), $100,170 (3), $113,580 (4). Among the highest in the nation. Full means test guide.

Does the PFD count?

The PFD's treatment is debated. Some courts include it; others exclude it. Consult an Alaska bankruptcy attorney.

What if I fail?

File Chapter 13 (no means test), wait for income to drop, challenge with special circumstances, or re-examine deductions.
