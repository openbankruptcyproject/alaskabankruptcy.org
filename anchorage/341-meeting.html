341 Meeting of Creditors in Anchorage

The 341 meeting is a brief hearing where the bankruptcy trustee asks you questions about your petition. Alaska uniquely accommodates telephonic appearances due to the state's vast distances.

What Is the 341 Meeting?

The 341 meeting of creditors is required in every bankruptcy case. Despite its name, creditors rarely attend. The trustee verifies your identity, confirms the accuracy of your petition, and asks basic questions about your assets, debts, and income. Your attorney attends with you.

What to Bring

  • Government-issued photo ID -- driver's license, passport, or state ID
  • Proof of Social Security number -- Social Security card, W-2, or tax return
  • Your attorney -- will attend with you and prepare you for questions

Telephonic Meetings in Alaska

Alaska is unique -- telephonic and video 341 meetings are routine, not exceptional. This is due to the state's geography.

  • Anchorage residents: Typically in person, remote options available
  • Fairbanks: Usually by phone/video (360+ miles from courthouse)
  • Juneau: Typically by phone/video (no road to Anchorage)
  • Rural communities: Almost always remote

Contact your trustee to arrange a telephonic appearance if travel is a hardship.

Typical Questions

  1. Did you review your petition before filing?
  2. Is everything listed accurate and complete?
  3. Have you listed all your assets and debts?
  4. Have you transferred property in the last two years?
  5. Do you expect an inheritance in the next 180 days?
  6. Are you current on tax filings?
  7. Do you have domestic support obligations?

Answer honestly and briefly. If unsure, say so.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is the meeting held?

605 W 4th Ave, Room 138, Anchorage, AK 99501. Conducted by the trustee, not a judge.

Can I attend by phone?

Yes. Telephonic appearances are routine in Alaska. Contact your trustee to arrange.

What if I miss it?

It will be rescheduled. Missing the rescheduled meeting may result in case dismissal. Contact your attorney immediately if you cannot attend.

Do creditors show up?

Rarely. In most consumer cases, no creditors attend.
