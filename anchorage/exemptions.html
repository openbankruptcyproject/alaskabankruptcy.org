Bankruptcy Exemptions in Anchorage, Alaska

Alaska allows you to choose between state and federal bankruptcy exemptions. This guide compares both sets so you can understand which better protects your property.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

State vs. Federal Exemptions

Alaska is one of approximately 20 states that allow filers to choose between state and federal exemptions. You must pick one complete set - you cannot mix items from both.

When to Choose Each Set

Choose State Exemptions When:

  • You own a home with equity above $27,900 (state homestead is $72,900)
  • You have valuable tools of trade (state: $4,050 vs. federal: $2,800)
  • You want to protect your PFD explicitly

Choose Federal Exemptions When:

  • You rent or have little/no home equity - the unused homestead converts to wildcard (up to $15,425 total)
  • You have significant household goods (federal: $14,875 vs. state: $4,050)
  • You need a larger vehicle exemption ($4,450 vs. $4,060)

Alaska-Specific Considerations

  • Homestead declaration: File a homestead declaration with the recorder's office to claim the full $72,900 state exemption. Without it, protection may be limited.
  • PFD in Chapter 13: The trustee may require your annual PFD as disposable income during your plan period.
  • Married couples: Joint filers can each claim a full set of exemptions, effectively doubling protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I choose between state and federal exemptions?

Yes. Alaska allows this choice. You must use one complete set. An experienced attorney can determine which protects more of your property. Full Alaska exemptions guide.

What is Alaska's homestead exemption?

$72,900 under state exemptions. Record a homestead declaration for full protection. Federal alternative is $27,900.

Is my PFD protected?

Generally yes in Chapter 7 under state exemptions. In Chapter 13, the trustee may require it as disposable income. Under federal exemptions, use your wildcard.
