Chapter 7 Bankruptcy in Anchorage, Alaska

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debt in 3-4 months. Anchorage residents file in the District of Alaska and can choose between state and federal exemptions to protect their property.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

What Chapter 7 Does

Chapter 7 is a liquidation bankruptcy that eliminates most unsecured debts. A court-appointed trustee reviews your assets, and any non-exempt property can be sold to pay creditors. In practice, most Chapter 7 cases are "no-asset" cases -- meaning the filer keeps everything because all property is protected by exemptions.

Debts Chapter 7 Eliminates

  • Credit card balances
  • Medical bills
  • Personal loans and payday loans
  • Old utility bills
  • Most lawsuit judgments
  • Deficiency balances after repossession or foreclosure

Debts Chapter 7 Does NOT Eliminate

  • Student loans (except in rare hardship cases)
  • Recent income taxes (generally last 3 years)
  • Child support and alimony
  • Court-ordered restitution
  • Debts from fraud or willful injury

The Means Test in Alaska

The means test determines whether you qualify for Chapter 7. Alaska's median incomes are among the highest in the nation due to the cost of living.

Step 1: Calculate your average monthly income over the past 6 months and annualize it.

Step 2: Compare to Alaska's median income for your household size. If your income is at or below the median, you qualify automatically.

Step 3: If above median, subtract allowable expenses. If the remainder is low enough, you still qualify.

Alaska's higher median income thresholds mean more people qualify for Chapter 7 here than in many other states.

Chapter 7 Timeline in Anchorage

  1. Pre-filing credit counseling -- Required within 180 days before filing ($15-25).
  2. File petition -- Filed at the District of Alaska, 605 W 4th Ave. The automatic stay takes effect immediately.
  3. 341 meeting of creditors -- 30-40 days after filing. Held at the courthouse in Anchorage. Telephonic appearances available for residents in remote areas.
  4. Debtor education course -- Must be completed before discharge ($10-25).
  5. Discharge -- Typically about 60 days after the 341 meeting. Total time: approximately 3-4 months from filing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I qualify for Chapter 7 in Anchorage?

You qualify if your household income is below Alaska's median income for your household size. Alaska's medians are higher than most states, so more people qualify. If above median, a detailed expense calculation may still qualify you. Learn about the means test.

What property can I keep in Chapter 7?

Alaska lets you choose state or federal exemptions. State exemptions protect $72,900 in home equity, $4,060 in vehicle equity, $4,050 in household goods, and $1,350 wildcard. Retirement accounts are fully exempt under both sets. Full exemptions guide.

How long does Chapter 7 take?

From filing to discharge, Chapter 7 typically takes 3-4 months. The 341 meeting occurs about 30-40 days after filing, and the discharge follows about 60 days after that.

Will I lose my car?

Alaska's state vehicle exemption is $4,060. If your car equity is below that, you keep it. If you owe money on the car and are current on payments, you can usually keep it by signing a reaffirmation agreement. The federal exemption offers $4,450 for a vehicle.
