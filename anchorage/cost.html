How Much Does Bankruptcy Cost in Anchorage?

A complete breakdown of filing fees, attorney fees, and other costs for bankruptcy in Anchorage. Alaska's higher cost of living affects attorney rates.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Fee Waivers and Payment Plans

Fee Waiver (Chapter 7 Only)

If your household income is below 150% of the federal poverty guidelines, you may qualify for a complete waiver of the $338 filing fee. File Form 103B with your petition.

Installment Payments

Pay the filing fee in up to 4 installments over 120 days. File Form 103A.

Chapter 13 Through Plan

In Chapter 13, filing fees and attorney fees can typically be paid through your repayment plan, minimizing upfront costs.

Ways to Reduce Costs

  • Alaska Legal Services Corporation - Free legal help for qualifying low-income residents. Call (907) 272-9431.
  • Online credit counseling - Approved online courses are typically cheaper than in-person.
  • Compare attorneys - Get quotes from multiple attorneys. Many offer free initial consultations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Chapter 7 cost in Anchorage?

Filing fee $338, attorney fees $1,500-$3,000, counseling $25-50. Total: $1,850-$3,400. Full cost guide.

Can I get a fee waiver?

If your income is below 150% of poverty, you may qualify for a complete waiver of the Chapter 7 filing fee. Installment payments are also available.

Why are fees higher in Alaska?

Alaska has the highest cost of living in the U.S., which affects attorney overhead. Filing fees are the same nationwide ($338/$313).
