Anchorage Bankruptcy FAQ

Common questions about filing bankruptcy in Anchorage, Alaska, including Alaska-specific issues like the Permanent Fund Dividend and telephonic hearings.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

General Questions

Where do I file for bankruptcy in Anchorage?

All Anchorage residents file in the District of Alaska at 605 W 4th Ave, Room 138, Anchorage, AK 99501. Alaska has only one federal bankruptcy district covering the entire state.

Should I file Chapter 7 or Chapter 13?

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debt in 3-4 months but requires a means test. Chapter 13 lets you keep property while repaying over 3-5 years. Compare the two.

How long does bankruptcy take?

Chapter 7: 3-4 months. Chapter 13: 3-5 years. The 341 meeting is about 30-40 days after filing.

How long does it stay on my credit report?

Chapter 7: 10 years. Chapter 13: 7 years. Most people can begin rebuilding credit within 1-2 years. Rebuilding credit guide.

Alaska-Specific Questions

Will I lose my PFD if I file bankruptcy?

In Chapter 7, your Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend is generally exempt under state exemptions. In Chapter 13, the trustee may require your PFD each year as disposable income. Discuss with your attorney before filing.

Can I choose between state and federal exemptions?

Yes. Alaska allows this choice. You must use one complete set. Compare state vs. federal exemptions.

Can I attend my 341 meeting by phone?

Yes. The District of Alaska regularly allows telephonic and video appearances due to Alaska's vast distances. Contact your trustee to arrange a remote appearance. Full 341 meeting guide.

Property and Assets

Can I keep my car?

Alaska's state vehicle exemption protects $4,060 in equity; federal protects $4,450. If your equity is below the exemption, you keep it. If making payments and current, you can reaffirm the debt. Keep your car guide.

Can I keep my house?

Alaska's state homestead exemption protects $72,900 in equity. In Chapter 13, you can keep your home while catching up on missed payments. Keep your house guide.

Can I file without an attorney?

Yes, but it is not recommended. Alaska Legal Services Corporation provides free help. Call (907) 272-9431.
